My primary role throughout the duration of this project was to implement front-end views and components for the patient-intake and follow-up survey slides. Graphic design mock-ups of every view and component were issued out by team lead Lee Saynor, and I was to try and bring them to life and make them usable the best of my ability. Lead Developer Daniel Tabak maintained a unique and frenetic pace throughout every milestone.

The tech stack was and largely still is on the bleeding edge of web development today - ES6 React paired with Redux and styled with the Materialize CSS framework alongside Swiper providing cohesion and kinetics for the whole show (the latter 2 coincidentally being used on this very website). On the back-end, everything sat on top Node.js with data models being managed by MongoDB and Mongoose.js.

Taking on multiple roles in this project, my contributions involved server setup with Node and Express.js, implementing a rudimentary front-to-back authentication module using JSON Web Tokens, as well as being largely responsible for the style guide and appearance of the front-end view elements.

This project is a very classic case of open-source methodology done right - from the coding rules agreed upon by the entire team, to everyone equally contributing to the documentation. Most importantly, this was a very fast-paced learning experience that focused on everyone being out of their comfort zone and coping with brand new tech stacks and frameworks, with me personally confronting React.js and JWTs for the first time.
